fun
Designing a regression detector that works without ground-truth labels - using embedding drift, semantic similarity clustering, and alert heuristics - is a real ML systems problem.
why now
Model providers are shipping updates faster than ever - gpt-4o-mini, claude-haiku, Gemini Flash variants all break prompts silently, and indie devs have no safety net.
why you
You build agentic systems in Atelier and feel this pain yourself - you can design the SDK from the perspective of someone who ships AI features, not an ML researcher.
wedge
A 20-line SDK: log your LLM calls, auto-compare outputs against a golden set weekly, send a Slack/email alert if semantic similarity drops below threshold. No ML expertise required.
rivals
Braintrust, PromptFoo, LangSmith, Evals.ai - all target ML teams, not indie builders
Pre-mortemBraintrust and LangSmith are well-funded and actively shipping simpler onboarding - the indie gap may close before you get traction.
First move. Post on HN 'Ask HN: How do you detect when a model update broke your AI feature?' - measure pain depth and learn current workarounds before building.
